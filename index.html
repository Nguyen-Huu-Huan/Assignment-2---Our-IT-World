Artificial Intelligence

  Artificial Intelligence refers to computers/machines whose capability allows them to perform complex tasks that can only be done with human intelligence. The state of the art of Artificial Intelligence relies on one of its word components: Intelligence. What is the definition of Intelligence? John McCarthy, the father of AI, has his answer for the question, saying: "Intelligence is the computational part of the ability to achieve goals in the world" [3]. Traditionally, computers solve problems by using developers and coders' instructions. Nowadays, with the help of AI, coders can teach computers how to become an independent thinker with its own Intelligence using algorithms such as Reinforcement Learning algorithm, Neural Network, etc. There are diverse applications of AI in our Vietnamese society such as voice, image recognition, spam email filtering, AI robot (e.g., Trí Nhân robot from ), AI X-ray Reader.

AI is classified into 3 levels of intelligence:

  • Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI): This is also known as a Weak AI. Some examples of this type of AI are Open AI - an AI bot that dominates even the top professional Dota player in 1v1 fights, DeepBlue - an AI bot that is capable of beating the world's greatest chess player Garry Kasparov, Pluribus - an AI bot that dominates over top professional pokers in a six-player game in Texas. All these examples have the same common ground which is its specialization: one AI for solely one field. This common ground is what distinguishes Weak AI from the 2 other higher levels of AI. Comparing to human, Weak AI may be the dominant on the spectrum of its specialized area, but other spectrums are too overwhelm for Weak AI's capability.
  • Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): This is also known as a Strong AI. Strong AI, by definition, can perform with human-level intelligence in any spectrum. In other words, no matter if the spectrum relates to politics, education, games, etc., Strong AI's knowledge covers them all. Human knowledge nowadays are insufficient to produce such AI. However, there are some notable attempts trying to reach this AI levels such as Sofia - a robot whose intelligence allows her to hold the title the first robot citizen in the world and many more similar unofficial versions.
  • Artificial Superintelligence (ASI): This is also known as a Super AI. Super AI is superior to Strong AI as it could surpass any existing human in the world in all areas. Machine Learning

  ML is one of the greatest applications of Probability and Statistics. Machine Learning uses principles of Probability and Statistics to process and extract the trends of known data (as "knowledge") for predicting future unknown data. For example, a table of columns (name, gender, age, favorite movie type) is fed to a machine learning model where name, gender, and age data are used as the model's column input and favorite movie type is the prediction output of the model. With enough amount of sample data (i.e., training data) given to the model, it will be able to predict what is the favorite movie type of a given set of information (e.g., Name: Bob, gender: Male, Age: 29). The fundamental process of applying machine learning includes:

  1. Get existing datasets (e.g., from the Internet, Organizations/Companies, etc.) or crawl data from these sources.
  2. Gather and turn the data into tabular format and perform data cleaning & processing using data manipulation techniques. In a table, some columns are unnecessary so they can be dropped. In some cases, data in desired columns are null or not presented in the right format, they have to be fixed before proceeding to the next step.
  3. Data visualization using processed data from the previous step. In other words, draw charts to present data in a more comprehensive way. Afterwards, identify anomalies and outliers (if exists) in the visualization before heading to the next steps.
  4. Use a set of columns as parameters for the machine learning model to take into account and choose a target column for the model to predict.
  5. Split data into training and testing with proportion specified (e.g, 70% for training and 30% for testing). Use appropriate pre-built machine learning models or re-build a new/existing one and feed data to these models for prediction.
  6. Use metrics for comparison between the models/algorithms' accuracy of prediction (i.e., compare how accurate the prediction of testing data is with respect to the original data) and choose the algorithm with the highest score.

Blockchain

  Blockchain is a one-of-a-kind database where all new data and information are electronically stored in chunks or blocks or a list of records. Each block contains its own hashed address (i.e., encrypted address), transaction data (e.g., how much money is sent from the sender to the recipient), and a timestamp denoting when the transaction was performed. There are two types of block, which are filled and unfilled. Filled blocks are saturated of data & information so they will have their own timestamp information and transaction data stored. For flexible backward traversing, Blockchain has these filled blocks chained together using links that consists of a hash code for the previous block. If new data comes in and there is no room left for storage, a new block will be formed to contain such data and it will become an unfilled block. Unfilled blocks (i.e., new or partially full blocks) are capable of storing more data before becoming a filled block. The process keeps going until the database exceeds the computing and computational power of computers. The state of the art of Blockchain is its decentralized architecture and distributed Peer-to-Peer (P2P) network. While traditional databases are operated by several centralized servers across the globe and each server requires an extensive number of computers (for computing power), Blockchain does not require such servers' locations to store data, and instead, it uses data shared by the P2P network.

Another challenging aspect of his job is to get unbiased label data.
    • Sherry List - Azure Developer Engagement Lead Her biggest challenge is that she does not have access to the right resources for her job due to working in Phillipine. Even when she went to Denmark, a developed nation, she reaffirmed that her access to learning is still insufficient and hard to find.
    • Jane Lew - Google Software Engineer When YK, the interviewer, asked Jane whether she had ever experienced the "infamous" imposter syndrome (also known as the syndrome of individuals who constantly doubt their abilities, skills and knowledge no matter how good they are) that many Google employees have while working at Google, she said yes. She described her anxiety of not having sufficient contribution to Google when she first and officially landed a job at Google.
    • Gandhar - ZX Security Penetration Tester He pointed out some of the challenges he face during his working hour such as physical exhaustion & fatigueness (due to working overtime), learning too many new things for each of the assigned projects. The most challenging, he emphasized, is the vast amount of technical knowledge required for breaking down the systems. This is because some of the customers' infrastructures have a firewall system incorporated, the breaking tasks can be halted due to the firewall's blockage. To deal with this hardship, he recommended viewers to "have that gut to just keep going".
